Crave

Swipe-Based Food Discovery App

Tools Used: Figma

Overview

Crave is a conceptual mobile app designed to help users discover food based on cravings and emotional states, not just cuisine types. Borrowing the addictive swipe gesture from Tinder, Crave transforms food discovery into a playful, sensory-first experience that prioritizes visuals, moods, and delight.

This project was developed as a self-initiated UX experiment to explore how emotional design and unconventional interactions can reshape everyday decisions — like “What should I eat?”

Problem Statement

Traditional food delivery apps often force users to choose by cuisine or restaurant. But what about those moments when you don’t know what you want but you just know you want something sweet, creamy, or spicy?

How might we help users discover food based on cravings and emotions, rather than rigid categories and logical filters?

Target Audience

  • Gen Z and Millennials who order food impulsively or emotionally
  • Late-night snackers, busy students, indecisive scrollers
  • People who love trying new dishes based on mood or sensory appeal

Pain Points

  • Too many filters, not enough fun
  • Decision fatigue
  • Inability to find “just the right thing” when craving something specific but vague

UX Process

1
Empathize
  • Quick user interviews revealed that most people often “feel like” something sweet, crunchy, warm rather than having a dish in mind
  • Common emotions tied to food cravings: comfort, boredom, celebration, late-night guilt, or stress
2
Define

User Goals

  • Quick, intuitive food discovery
  • Less cognitive load
  • A fun, visual interaction (something scroll fatigue can’t offer)

How Might We...

  • …match users with dishes that reflect their current craving or mood?
  • …turn food discovery into a game-like experience?
3
Ideate
  • Borrowed swipe UX from Tinder but applied it to dishes
  • Designed filters for “Mood” instead of just meal-type (e.g., Cozy, Energized, Bold, Melty)
  • Used sensory triggers (images, textures) instead of just names and descriptions
4
Design
  • Created high-fidelity wireframes in Figma
  • Defined a bright and bold color palette with warm accents and gradient overlays to signal flavor and mood
  • Designed playful dish cards with microcopy like “Feeling Cheesy?”, “Get Saucy”, “Crunch Attack”
5
Prototype
  • Figma Smart Animate used to create seamless card transitions
  • Mood filter transitions animated with blur and softness for emotional impact
6
Test
  • Usability tests with 5 users (ages 19–26)
  • Feedback: “Super fun”, “love the vibe”, “makes me hungry lol”, “could get addictive”
  • Iterated swipe delay timing and added "undo" for accidental skips

Visual Design

Crave’s visual identity blends warmth, flavor, and fun — echoing the spontaneity of street food and the addictiveness of swipe-based interfaces.

Signup Screen – Select Your Preferred Cuisine

The onboarding experience begins with a taste-first approach — users pick their favorite cuisines upfront. Large, tappable cards and a bright CTA make the process intuitive and snackable.

Home Screen – Welcome Back, Pav

The home screen surfaces dishes and restaurants nearby dynamically, with clean icon-based navigation and subtle personal touches for a welcoming user experience.

Swiping Screen – Swipe, Don’t Scroll

One dish at a time. Swipe left to skip or right to crave. Inspired by Tinder, this reduces choice overload and makes food discovery more fun and spontaneous.

Craving Setter – What’s Your Vibe Today?

Use sliders like Spicy, Refreshing, Sweet, and Comforting to define your current craving. A sensory-first approach to navigating food moods.

Saved Screen – Your Cravings, Collected

Every right swipe gets saved here. A visual recipe board of all your past cravings, helping build a personal flavor library over time.

Profile Screen – Personalized & Adaptable

Edit your food preferences, manage your cuisines, and control your flavor journey. The profile reinforces user agency and personalization.

Final Design

  • Swipe-First UX: One dish at a time. Left to skip, right to crave.
  • Mood Filters: Select how you feel, not what you want.
  • Dynamic Dish Cards: With textures, steam effects, and microcopy
  • Crave Collection: Saved dishes auto-sorted by mood

Outcomes and Learnings

  • Designed a system that centers emotion-driven interaction over conventional logic
  • Learned to balance playfulness with usability because fun shouldn’t sacrifice clarity
  • Future idea: Integrate with food delivery APIs for a real-world MVP

Reflections

Crave was a playground for designing with the senses like flavor, feel, and fun. It challenged me to think beyond functional UX and into the realm of delight and desire. The swiping may feel silly at first, but that’s the point: food should feel good.
