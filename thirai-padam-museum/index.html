Thirai Padam: Kollywood Museum App

Preserving the Art of Tamil Cinema in a Digital Space

Overview

Thirai Padam is a conceptual mobile application crafted to celebrate and archive the richness of Kollywood — the Tamil film industry. The app functions as a digital museum, spotlighting directors, cinematographers, music composers, and cult classics with an immersive and curated experience. Designed to evoke nostalgia while maintaining modern usability, Thirai Padam bridges generations of cinephiles by making film history both accessible and interactive.

Problem Statement

Despite Tamil cinema's global influence, there is no centralized or digital-first platform that preserves and curates its artistic, cultural, and cinematic legacy. The challenge was to design an immersive, emotionally engaging experience that documents, educates, and inspires through the art of film without turning into a dry repository or feeling like a streaming platform.

Target Audience

  • Tamil cinema enthusiasts and nostalgic cinephiles
  • Film students and scholars studying Indian cinema
  • Young audiences discovering Kollywood classics
  • Design and media researchers exploring regional film heritage

Tech Stack

  • Figma (UX/UI Design & Prototyping)
  • Maze (Usability Testing)
  • Google Fonts (Newsreader, EB Garamond, Crimson Text)
  • Canva (Presentation Mockups)

Core Features

  • Home Feed: Daily highlights, featured artworks, and historical tidbits about classic Tamil cinema.
  • Discover Tab: Curated film posters, mood-based filters (nostalgic, dramatic, romantic, etc.), and cinematic trivia.
  • Artists Tab: Pages dedicated to iconic directors, actors, cinematographers, and composers. Each artist profile includes timelines, notable works, and visual galleries.
  • Cue Cut Tab: Deep-dives into specific scenes, shot breakdowns, cinematographic elements, color palette analysis, and emotional tone interpretation.
  • Gallery: High-res visuals, frame grabs from legendary scenes, storyboard sketches, and fan submissions.

Visual Design

Thirai Padam’s visual identity is a love letter to Tamil cinema. Rooted in vintage aesthetics and archival elegance, the design blends the richness of #5B2C35 and #BD8C67 with textured backgrounds and serif-heavy typography (Newsreader, EB Garamond, Crimson Text) to craft an experience that feels as timeless as the films it celebrates. Each screen plays like a frame from a classic film — thoughtful, artful, and soaked in nostalgia.

Onboarding – Enter the Archive

The app opens with poetic motion graphics and a minimalist welcome message: “Cinema is not just watched. It is felt.” Users are prompted to choose their interests: eras, directors, genres, or iconic moments. This early personalization ensures that every scroll ahead is curated to the user’s cinephile soul.

  • Customizable onboarding paths — emotion-driven or decade-based
  • Smooth sepia-tone transitions to set the museum-like tone

Home – A Curated Film Museum

The home screen is a living exhibit. From dynamic carousels of iconic posters to featured director highlights, users are welcomed into a richly designed digital museum. Each tile features metadata like year, language, and genre.

  • Curated sections: “Today in Cinema,” “Unsung Films,” “Frames of the 80s”
  • Tap-to-zoom interaction on poster thumbnails

Discover – Cinema Through Moods & Genres

Not just genres. Not just actors. Discover is about emotion. From filters like “Bittersweet Endings” to tags like “Visually Lyrical,” this screen invites users to explore cinema as an emotional journey — one screen at a time.

  • Advanced filters: genre, year, mood, aesthetic, language
  • Adaptive grid for mobile and tablet devices

Artist Profiles – The Maestros of Thirai

Each artist page is a deep dive into vision. With elegant parallax scrolling, users move through a timeline of that artist’s filmography, quotes, visuals, and collaborations.

  • Color palette analysis from iconic scenes
  • Poster evolution of each film over time
  • Director/actor collaboration charts

Cue Cut – Iconic Cinematic Moments

Think of it as the director’s cut, but visual. Cue Cut is a reel of stills, gifs, and cinematography breakdowns. Whether it’s the golden light from Nayakan or the silhouette frames from Aruvi, this section deconstructs visual language.

  • Frame-by-frame commentary
  • Cinematographer notes and trivia
  • Color grading side-by-sides (original vs remastered)

Gallery – A Poster Lover’s Paradise

The gallery is a digital vault of rare poster art, behind-the-scenes stills, lobby cards, and hand-drawn film illustrations. A full-screen image viewer allows users to zoom, save, and favorite artworks.

  • Poster search by artist or year
  • Aesthetic filter: monochrome, illustrated, minimal
  • Hover metadata and download option

UX Process

1
Empathize
Interviews with cinephiles, professors of film studies, and local theatre owners revealed the emotional attachment audiences have with visual storytelling and nostalgia.
2
Define
Defined core needs: reverence for cultural roots, seamless storytelling, archival accuracy, and engaging navigation across themes and decades.
3
Ideate
Explored museum-inspired layouts, poster-grid concepts, film reel UI scrolls, and character-driven interactions (like avatar cues from directors).
4
Prototype
Built high-fidelity flows in Figma, incorporating cinematic motion, rich color palettes, and textured overlays inspired by aged film reels.
5
Test
Usability testing via Maze showed strong emotional response. Iterations focused on enhancing readability and ensuring intuitive filter logic in Discover tab.

Final Design

The final prototype delivers a nostalgic, film-reel-inspired mobile interface that balances informational depth with cultural reverence. Whether browsing by genre or deep-diving into a legendary shot, every screen aims to feel like opening a personal scrapbook of Tamil cinema history. Features like scrollable director timelines, fan-submitted galleries, and trivia overlays enrich user engagement and preserve storytelling traditions.

Outcomes & Learnings

  • Recognized the emotional power of regional cinema design and how aesthetics can support cultural preservation.
  • Validated the use of sepia tones and aged film textures through usability testing — users described the interface as “museum-like” and “personal.”
  • Learned how to curate information density without overwhelming the user — balancing visuals, timelines, and interactive cues for a cinematic archive.

Conclusion

Thirai Padam is more than a digital archive — it's a tribute to Tamil cinema’s heritage and craftsmanship. Through a blend of rich design, cultural cues, and emotional storytelling, this app makes cinema appreciation feel personal, immersive, and timeless.
